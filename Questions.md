# In Search of Dharma — Question Registry

This is the **master registry** and **status dashboard** for the DD research project.
Each question has a stable ID (`category.question`), maps to exactly one research note
(`N-<category>/<id>-<slug>.md`), and moves through the status vocabulary
`todo` → `in-progress` → `review` → `done`. The per-question workflow is in
[`METHODOLOGY.md`](METHODOLOGY.md); the note structure is
[`1-foundational/_template.md`](1-foundational/_template.md).

32 core questions (4 per category), plus extension notes where a topic merits its own
cited note (e.g. `4.5`).

## 1 — Foundational

### 1.1 Core Etymology
Trace the etymology of 'dharma' beyond Sanskrit to Proto-Indo-European roots.
What cognitive and social functions do these linguistic origins reveal about
humanity's need for ethical frameworks?

### 1.2 Universal Patterns
How does dharma represent a universal cognitive pattern that emerges independently
across cultures? What evolutionary pressures drive humans to create dharmic frameworks?

### 1.3 Boundary & Definition
What makes something a *dharma* as opposed to mere custom, law, etiquette, or
ideology? Where are the boundaries of the concept, and what is the minimal set of
features a framework must have to count as dharmic?

### 1.4 Cross-cultural Cognates
How does dharma compare with cognate ordering concepts from other traditions —
Greek *nomos* and *logos*, Chinese *Tao*, Vedic *ṛta*, Egyptian *ma'at*, and Western
*natural law*? What is genuinely shared across them, and what is distinct to each?

---

## 2 — Historical

### 2.1 Axial Age Catalyst
What specific socio-economic and psychological pressures during the Axial Age
(800–200 BCE) catalyzed the simultaneous emergence of dharmic thinking across
disconnected civilizations?

### 2.2 Urban Complexity
How did the transition from kinship-based tribes to anonymous urban societies
necessitate explicit dharmic codes? What problems, individual and societal, did these
frameworks seek to solve?

### 2.3 Pre-Axial / Pre-literate Ethics
What was the ethical baseline before codified dharmas? How did pre-Axial, pre-literate
societies regulate moral behaviour without explicit doctrine, and what does that
baseline tell us about what the Axial codifications actually added?

### 2.4 Transmission & Institutionalization
How did dharmas spread, ossify, and get carried across generations — by priesthoods,
monastic and philosophical schools, scribes, and states? What happens to a dharma when
it becomes institutionalized?

---

## 3 — Philosophical

### 3.1 Tension Resolution
How do dharmic systems navigate the fundamental tension between individual autonomy and
collective welfare? Provide specific examples of how different traditions resolve this.

### 3.2 Metacognitive Function
What role does dharma play in human metacognition — our ability to think about
thinking? How do dharmic practices shape cognitive development?

### 3.3 The Grounding Problem
Can a secular dharma be normatively binding without metaphysical foundations? How do
dharmic frameworks confront the is/ought gap, and what grounds their authority once
divine command is removed?

### 3.4 Relativism vs. Universalism
If dharmas are genuinely plural, are some better than others — and by what standard?
How do dharmic frameworks avoid collapsing into relativism while resisting a single
imposed universalism?

---

## 4 — Cultural

### 4.1 Samin of Java
Explain the origins, beliefs, and dharma of the Samin of Java. How does it differ from
traditional Javanese culture?

### 4.2 Ubuntu
Analyze Ubuntu ('I am because we are') as a dharmic framework. How does it differ from
individualistic dharmas while serving similar social functions?

### 4.3 Indigenous Oral Dharmas
Examine indigenous dharmic systems (e.g. Native American, Aboriginal Australian) that
predate written traditions. What can oral dharmas teach us about essential vs. cultural
elements?

### 4.4 Abrahamic Ethics as Dharmic Frameworks
Analyze Abrahamic ethical systems — covenant, halakha, sharia, Christian caritas — as
dharmic frameworks. What does the non-privileging stance require us to see in them, and
how do they function like and unlike Indic / secular dharmas?

### 4.5 Subud and Sumarah
What is the Javanese Subud movement — its origins, beliefs and practice (the *latihan
kejiwaan*)? Who practises it and how widely? How does it relate to the project's dharma
concepts (its very name embeds *dharma* — **Su**sila **Bu**dhi **D**harma)? And how does it
compare with the smaller, quietist Sumarah movement it resembles? *(Extension note — a
Javanese theistic-mysticism boundary case, sibling to 4.1 Samin.)*

### 4.6 Badui of West Java
Who are the Badui (Urang Kanekes) of West Java — their culture, beliefs (*Sunda Wiwitan*)
and common practices (the *pikukuh* customary code)? What dharma analogues do they exhibit
(non-alteration of the world, asceticism, forest guardianship), and how do these relate to
the project's dharma concepts? How do they compare with the Samin (4.1) and indigenous oral
dharmas (4.3)? *(Extension note — an indigenous Sundanese boundary case.)*

## 5 — Psychological & Neuroscientific

### 5.1 Neurological Basis
What neuroscientific evidence supports dharmic intuitions about interconnectedness,
compassion, and ethical behaviour? How do contemplative practices alter brain structure?

### 5.2 Evolutionary Psychology
From an evolutionary psychology perspective, how do dharmic frameworks exploit cognitive
biases and social instincts to promote cooperation? What are the adaptive advantages?

### 5.3 Moral Psychology & Development
What does moral psychology (e.g. Haidt's moral foundations, Kohlberg's stages of moral
development) and the WEIRD-sample critique tell us about how dharmic intuitions form and
vary across populations?

### 5.4 Ritual, Practice & Habituation
How do dharmas install themselves behaviourally? What is the role of ritual, repeated
practice, and habituation in turning a dharmic ideal into a lived disposition?

### 5.5 Psychopaths & the Exploiter Limit-Case
Examine the role of what we call *psychopaths* in human development, history, and evolution.
What is this human type, what evolutionary and neural dynamics produce it, how have
cooperative/dharmic systems policed it, and what does it reveal about the limits of moral
psychology? *(Extension note — the exploiter limit-case that cooperative orders evolved to
police, widened to the double-edged "functional minority": the low-fear boldness dimension
(fearless dominance) evolution kept as a low-frequency niche, and why any group-level benefit
is contested. Sibling to 5.2 evolutionary-psychology and 7.1 failure-modes; the essay limit-case
"The Missing Elephant" in Part 5.)*

### 5.6 Neurodivergence (umbrella)
What is "neurodivergence" — a natural kind or a contested umbrella — and what place do atypical
minds hold in the human group? Do cultures channel cognitive variation into sanctioned specialist
niches (seer, shaman, systemiser, ascetic), and is that best read as an individual-level fact or a
plausibly group-level feature of human sociality? *(Extension note & index — frames the four
children below and positions 5.5 psychopaths as the exploitative instance of the same
cognitive-variation fact; inherits 5.2's group-selection hedge. Lives in the subdirectory
`5-psychological/5.6-neurodivergence/`.)*

### 5.6.1 Autism & the Systemising Mind
What does the evidence actually show about autism spectrum disorder and the "systemising mind" —
diagnostic history, ascertainment-driven prevalence, heritability, the empathising–systemising /
"extreme male brain" theory, savants — and where does "difference not deficit" hold or fail?
*(Extension note under 5.6 — the paradigm case; the trait (systemising) kept rigorously distinct
from the condition (autism).)*

### 5.6.2 The Contemplative Vocation
Are atypical minds genuinely drawn to, and over-represented in, the contemplative vocations
(monastic, mystical, shamanic, eremitic, ascetic)? Which temperaments fit which niches, and how
much of the popular "monks and mystics were neurodivergent" claim survives the evidence?
*(Extension note under 5.6 — the dharma-central, spiciest thread; the sourceable core is narrow,
and for autism the belief data run the OTHER way. Read against 5.6.1.)*

### 5.6.3 The Technical Vocation
Are systemising and autistic minds genuinely over-represented in programming and Silicon Valley —
what does the evidence show, how measurement-fragile is it, and what is the ethical double-edge of
treating the technical niche as a home for such minds? *(Extension note under 5.6 — "the niche
helps and uses in the same motion", reusing 5.5's double-edge logic.)*

### 5.6.4 Giftedness, Dyslexia & the Wider Spectrum
Beyond autism: giftedness and twice-exceptionality, dyslexia, ADHD, Gardner's multiple
intelligences, and benign variants such as synesthesia — how well do "difference not deficit" and
"hidden strengths" survive the evidence, and where does the dilution critique ("if everyone is
neurodivergent, no one is") bite? *(Extension note under 5.6 — the "and so on" catch-all; the
discipline cuts both ways. Hinges onto 7.4 measurement-falsifiability.)*

## 6 — Contemporary

### 6.1 Secular Translation
How can traditional dharmic insights be translated into secular frameworks without
losing their transformative power? What are successful examples?

### 6.2 Digital-Age Dharma
What new dharmic frameworks are emerging to address digital-age challenges like
information overload, social media, and AI? How do they differ from traditional forms?

### 6.3 Economics, Work & Inequality
How do dharmic frameworks engage material life — work, wealth, debt, and inequality?
How have dharmas justified, constrained, or critiqued economic arrangements?

### 6.4 Gender & Dharma
How do dharmic systems encode, enforce, or contest gender roles? Where have dharmas been
used to subordinate, and where to liberate, along gender lines?

---

## 7 — Critical

### 7.1 Failure Modes
What are the common failure modes of dharmic systems? How do they become corrupted,
co-opted by power structures, or turn exclusionary?

### 7.2 Cultural Adaptation & Appropriation
How can Western adoption of Eastern dharmic concepts avoid superficial appropriation
(such as orientalism) while enabling genuine cross-cultural learning?

### 7.3 Dharma & Power
How do dharmic frameworks both challenge and reinforce existing power structures? When
do they liberate vs. oppress? Are they inclusive or exclusive?

### 7.4 Measurement & Falsifiability
Can the efficacy of a dharma be empirically assessed? What would count as evidence that
one dharmic framework 'works' better than another — and what does answering this set as
the evidentiary bar for this project itself?

### 7.5 Orientalism
What is the concept of Orientalism (especially Edward Said's) — its core thesis, intellectual
roots and method, and the major critiques of it? How does it bear on the study, translation and
appropriation of "dharma" and "Eastern wisdom", and on this project's own standing as a
Western-curated, secular corpus of "world dharmas"? *(Extension note — the critical concept
underwriting 7.2's "such as orientalism"; the most self-implicating note in the set.)*

## 8 — Future

### 8.1 Planetary Dharma
As humanity faces global challenges (climate change, inequality, technological
disruption), what elements of a 'planetary dharma' are emerging?

### 8.2 AI & Dharma
How might artificial intelligence require new dharmic frameworks? What ethical principles
should guide human–AI coexistence — including economic disempowerment and Universal Basic
Income (UBI)?

### 8.3 Post-Traditional Dharmas
What would a post-traditional dharma look like — one that honours wisdom traditions while
embracing scientific understanding and cultural pluralism?

### 8.4 Encoding Dharma into Machines
Reflexive: how should a researched dharma corpus be distilled and encoded to ground
future LLMs in human ethics? What are the risks of flattening plural dharmas into a
single machine-readable ethic — the project's own end-goal under scrutiny?
