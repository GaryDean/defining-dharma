# Series Review — *In Search of Dharma*, Parts 0–8

_Run: 2026-07-06 · scope: the nine essays as a single work · method: full sequential
read (as a book reader would) + quantitative tic analysis of the essay bodies
(frontmatter → `<!--audio stop-->`), measured against the series' own rubric in
`_essay-template.md` and `Essays.md`._

_Relation to prior reviews: `FABLE-REVIEW.md` (2026-07-02) covered citation
integrity and note-level hygiene and confirmed the arc at a structural level. This
review does what that one did not: it judges the series as a **reading
experience** — coherence felt in sequence, repetition, rhythm, and voice. Citation
truth was not re-checked here._

_**Author decisions (2026-07-06).** (a) Essay length limits are lifted: an essay
may run as long or as short as it needs; the 5,000 ±1,000 budget is no longer a
constraint on revision. (b) The year-12 reading-level ceiling is relaxed: hard
concepts may stretch the reader, and undergraduate-register passages are not in
themselves defects. These decisions soften §4's register note and the length
component of recommendation #8 (P8's authority density remains the live concern,
not its word count). §5's mechanical fixes were applied the same day — see the
✓ marks below._

## 1. Verdict

**The series hangs together.** Read end to end it is one argument, not eight
essays in a costume: the five-beat arc (Dissolve → Establish → Stress-test →
Pivot → Build) is genuinely felt, all seven hand-offs land, all three planted
threads pay off, and the closing standard ("holds without crushing") is honestly
earned rather than asserted. The prose is consistently high quality — confident,
concrete, and unusually honest about its own method.

**It also has tics, and they are measurable.** The largest are: (a) a
compounding recap burden across the P5→P6 seam and the P1→P7 Euthyphro reprise;
(b) an over-reliance on the "not X but Y" reversal (~86 instances, roughly one
every 500 words); (c) a small set of pet adverbs and nouns ("quietly" ×24,
"machinery" ×19, "precisely" ×17); and (d) a second-person count in Parts 1–5
that runs four to six times over the template's own stated ceiling. None of these
is fatal; all are fixable with a targeted pass rather than a rewrite.

**It reads well** — but at a register closer to first-year university than the
declared year-12, and Part 8, the destination essay, is the one place where
density (20 "the-discipline-Name" introductions, 26 key works, 5,590 words)
risks fatiguing the reader exactly when the argument most needs them fresh.

## 2. Coherence: does the series hold as one work?

### ✓ What demonstrably works

**The arc closes.** Every hand-off connects to the next essay's opening, and the
phrasing varies enough that the machinery never clanks: P2's "watch one being
born" delivers the Samin; P4's "how it installs" delivers P5; P6's double
question (judgement + what now) is explicitly split across P7 and P8.

**All planted threads pay off, on schedule.**

| Thread | Planted | Paid off | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Can a godless dharma bind? | P1 "Two Questions I'm Leaving Open" | P7 "Can It Bind?" (Euthyphro + trilemma + Foot) | ✓ |
| Are some dharmas better? | P1 (promissory note: "plural ≠ anything goes") | P8 "Plural, but Not Anything Goes" (Berlin, Nussbaum, floor) | ✓ |
| The yoga-studio image | P1 opening | P6 "The Mirror: Dharma in the Yoga Studio" | ✓ |
| Sosis's "unpaid bill" (secular strictness deficit) | P2 "The Price of Belonging" | P7 "Can It Install?" | ✓ |
| The Sunday-Assembly question ("we will come back to it") | P5 | P8 (community deficit, loneliness data) | ✓ |

**An unadvertised fourth thread is one of the best things in the book.** The
intimidator-who-cannot-be-shamed is planted in P2 (Boehm's band and its last
resort), given its biology in P5 ("The Missing Elephant"), and lands in P6 ("The
Wolf in the Flock") as the predator the mega-society can no longer watch. This
three-essay slow burn is not in the `Essays.md` thread list, and it is executed
with more patience than the listed threads. Worth adding to the blueprint so it
is protected in future revision.

**Recurring figures deepen rather than repeat.** The Samin return in P6 (the same
machinery refusing vs sanctifying authority) and P8 (the commons scaled to a
planet; Paine's dividend as "the Samin's commons paid out in money") — each
return does new work. The seeker on the cushion binds P7 to P8's opening and to
its final paragraph. The root metaphor travels the whole distance: *that which
holds* (P1) → "what holds us next" (P8), extended into the judgement standard
"holds without crushing." This is what a series that trusts its own imagery looks
like.

**No doctrinal contradictions found.** The four-feature test, the
plural-unprivileged stance, the made-not-found thesis, and the thin-floor
resolution are applied consistently across all nine essays.

### ▲ Where coherence strains

**The P5→P6 seam carries the series' one real content duplication.**
_(✓ resolved 2026-07-06 — see recommendation #1.)_ P5's closing section ("The
Machine Has No Conscience") and P6's second section ("The Dark Twin") made the
same argument — the installation machinery is morally blank, grooves cruelty as
faithfully as kindness — in nearly the same terms, a few pages apart for a
sequential reader, including a near-verbatim repeat of the thesis sentence ("not
one of them has any moral content of its own"). P6's restatement now opens with
a two-sentence acknowledgment that leans on P5 by number ("Part 5 ended on the
blankness of the last of these…") instead of re-performing it; the fix also
removed a template-violating back-reference ("as we saw last time"). The
five-strengths enumeration and the four fresh dark-twin examples were kept —
they are the essay's schema, not duplication.

**Euthyphro is fully staged twice.** _(✓ resolved 2026-07-06 — see
recommendation #2.)_ P1 explained both horns of the dilemma at length; P7 said
"we already met" Plato's trap and then explained both horns at length again. P1
is now cut to the naming of the trap and the promise ("the trap he set there for
the divine lawgiver has never been cleanly escaped… I think there is a real
answer… it has to be earned"), which is all the plant requires; the dilemma is
worked exactly once, in P7 where the thread pays off. P7's back-reference was
adjusted to match ("the first of which Part 1 named and left standing" — echoing
P1's own "name them and deliberately leave them standing"), which also converts
a vague "earlier we looked at" into the template-preferred by-number reference.

**The cooperation thesis is expounded three times before Part 3.** P0 ("The One
Idea"), P1 ("Why Everyone Has (at Least) One"), and P2 (the whole essay) each
derive morality-as-cooperation-technology. Worse, one sentence is duplicated
almost verbatim: the toolkit list "reciprocity, reputation, gratitude, guilt, a
sharp/keen eye for unfairness, and a powerful instinct to learn and enforce the
local rules" appears in P0 ¶"The main problem is cooperation…" and again in P1
¶"Start with the problem every group has to solve…" with only "sharp"→"keen"
changed. If the echo is a deliberate drumbeat, it does not read as one — the
surrounding sentences also run parallel, so it reads as copy-paste. Vary one of
the two (P0 is the natural place, since the preface only needs the idea in
outline, not the full toolkit).

**Other double-exposures a cover-to-cover reader will notice** (each individually
defensible under the standalone rule; listed so revision can choose deliberately):

- Boehm's reverse dominance hierarchy: full treatment in P2, full-ish again in
  P3 ("I can only gesture at here" — then a complete explanation), brief in P6.
- McMindfulness: introduced with the same content (Purser, stripped ethics,
  productivity tool) in P6 and again in P7, consecutive essays.
- Cleckley/Hare and the ~1% figure: introduced in P5 and re-introduced in P6.
- Curry's seven rules: P1 (full), P2 (flagged back-reference — good model),
  P8 (full re-introduction).
- Parochial altruism / in-group–out-group coevolution: P2, P5 (oxytocin), P6.

**One small wobble in the callback machinery.** P8: "The whole standard fits in
the phrase this series began with. A dharma is to be judged by how well it *holds
without crushing*." The phrase the series began with is *that which holds*;
"holds without crushing" is its extension, coined here. A one-word repair ("fits
inside the phrase…" / "extends the phrase…") keeps the callback honest.

**Terminology drift in the four-feature test.** P1 names feature two
**internalisation** ("binds you from the inside… who to *become*"); P3 and the
`Essays.md` blueprint call it **identity-constituting**. Same substance, two
names, and P3 explicitly claims to be re-running "the test from the first part."
Pick one term and use it in both essays.

## 3. Repetitive tics: the measured inventory

### 3.1 Template compliance scorecard

The series ships its own style law in `_essay-template.md`. Measured against it
(essay bodies only):

| Rule | Limit | Measured (P0…P8) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Em dashes | 1–2 per essay | 2,2,2,2,2,1,0,0,0 | ✓ clean |
| Declared `words:` | measured, not guessed | all within 6 words of actual | ✓ clean |
| Short-sentence runs (3+ of ≤6 words) | never | 0 found | ✓ clean |
| Length | 5,000 ±1,000 | 4,085–5,590 (P0 exempt) | ✓ clean |
| Self-narration ("this essay/series/search") | "a couple of light touches" | 2,1,5,1,0,1,12 for P1–P8; P0=10 | ✓ mostly (P0/P8 exempt: preface and the Mirror section are *about* the project) |
| Stage-directions | banned list | 9 survivors: "I owe you" ×2 (P1, P3), "notice that/what/how/where" ×4 (P1, P2, P4 ×2), "Let me…" ×3 (P3 ×2, P0) | ▲ small residue |
| Second person | "well under ten per ~5,000 words" | **19, 44, 41, 48, 58, 42, 16, 18, 7** | ✗ Parts 1–5 → ✓ resolved 2026-07-06 by amending the rule (see §3.1 note) |

The second-person figure needs calibration rather than panic: the texture is
mostly the companionable generic-you ("you can hear the family resemblance"),
almost never the hectoring command the template calls the tic's worst form, and
some instances are load-bearing (P1's turn — "You are standing inside a dharma
right now" — should not be touched). But 44–58 against a stated ceiling of
"well under ten" is not a rounding error, and the clean compliance of P6–P8
(16, 18, 7) proves the voice survives the pruning. Either Parts 1–5 get a
generic-you pass (prefer "we"/impersonal, as the template says), or the template's
ceiling should be revised to match the practice the author actually wants. The
rule and the practice currently disagree by a factor of five; one of them should
move.

_**✓ Resolved 2026-07-06 by amending the law** (recommendation #5, author's
choice). The numeric ceiling is gone from `_essay-template.md`. The amended rule
codifies what the essays actually do: the generic, companionable second person
and the guide's invitations to move ("picture two worlds", "now travel back")
carry no ceiling; what remains forbidden is the hector ("you do not get to opt
out…") and the didactic push that orders the reader's attention or conclusions
("notice that…", "ask yourself…"). Measured against the amended rule, all nine
essays comply — the only textual violation, P1's "Ask yourself whether…", was
already removed by recommendation #2's Euthyphro cut. The small residue of
banned stage-directions ("I owe you" ×2, "notice…" ×4, "Let me…" ×3) remains
tracked under recommendation #10._

### 3.2 The signature construction: "not X but Y"

The reversal ("not a revelation but a response", "made, not found", "not the
absence of a dharma but the search for one") is the series' deepest rhetorical
groove:

| P0 | P1 | P2 | P3 | P4 | P5 | P6 | P7 | P8 | Total |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|-------|
| 8 | 9 | 7 | 7 | 12 | 13 | 4 | 12 | 16 | **~88** |

That is one reversal per ~500 words for nine consecutive essays. It is
thematically motivated — the whole argument is a reversal of what the reader
thinks dharma is — and the best instances are the series' best sentences. But at
this density the device stops registering as emphasis and starts registering as
default sentence shape; by P7–P8 an attentive reader can predict the second half
of a sentence from its first. Recommendation: a per-essay pass that keeps the
thesis-bearing reversals (perhaps a third of them) and recasts the incidental
ones, prioritising P4, P5, P7, P8 where density is highest.

_**✓ Pass applied 2026-07-06** (recommendation #3) to the four densest essays:
P4 12→8, P5 13→9, P7 12→7, P8 15→11 (P8's true pre-count was 15; one instance
hid inside another's grep context). Series total ~88 → **69**. In practice the
thesis-bearing share proved larger than the estimated third — most survivors are
definitional contrasts, quotations, or hand-off lines — so the pass targeted
(a) doubled sentences running the device twice (P4's "everywhen" and
kincentricity sentences; P8's floor pair), (b) cluster spikes (P7's "New
Terrain" had four in two paragraphs), and (c) echo-bugs (P8 ran "not sincerity
but…" twice; the closing instance was kept). Recasts were varied — "reaches
past", "far from", "worse than wrong:", "stops being… and becomes", active-verb
semicolons — so no single replacement shape becomes the new tic. One incidental
win: an "as we will see" signpost (P7) was removed in passing. The untouched
essays (P0–P3, P6) sit at 4–9 each, already below the revised density._

Totals across all nine bodies (~45,000 words):

|---|---|---|

| "machinery" | 19 | legitimate as P5's schema; leaks into P2 (×4), P6 (×5) where "apparatus/mechanism/toolkit" already rotate |
| "precisely" | 17 | often deletable without loss |

| "sting" (as noun of consequence) | 5 | "carries a sting in its tail" (P2), "the reflexive sting" (P4), "the sting I cannot avoid" (P6)… noticeable because the word is vivid |

None of these would be visible in a single essay. In a book read across a few
evenings, "quietly" and "not X but Y" will be the two things a sharp reviewer

_**✓ Pass applied 2026-07-06** (recommendation #4). Post-pass counts: "quietly"
24→16 (kept where covertness is the claim, cut where the sentence already
carried it); "precisely" 17→10 (kept on paradoxes, cut before ordinary
"because"); "turns out" 13→8 (kept genuine discovery moments; the P7 cut also
fixed an overclaim — the summary asserted what the body only offered as "may
be"); "worth X-ing" 16→4 (the two survivors are deliberate; the rest recast
with varied frames — "it pays to be precise", "one question presses on", direct
questions); "machinery" 19→16 (P5's schema and its P6 callbacks untouched;
P8's stray became "engine", continuing its own sentence's metaphor); "sting"
recast in P4 ("the argument bites its owner"), keeping P2's original and P6's
climactic use. Every instance was judged in context — no blind
search-and-replace._

### 3.4 Structural formulas (the rhythm-level tics)

These are per-essay *shapes* that repeat across the series:

1. **The contrasting-vignette cold open.** P2 ("Picture two human worlds"), P4
   ("Picture four people, in four worlds"), P8 ("Begin with two pictures, both
   true"), plus P5's triple vignette ("A man… Or: a woman… Or, simpler still:
   you…"). Four of nine openings run the same gambit, and P2/P4/P8 share the
   explicit "picture N things" instruction. Each works alone; in sequence the
   reader starts to see the card being palmed. P4 or P8 could open differently
   (P8's second picture — the Anthropic constitution — is strong enough to open
   cold, unannounced).

2. **The recap paragraph.** Every essay from P3 onward carries a "previously on"
   block in its second or third section ("We have already done some heavy
   lifting", "We have come some distance already", "In the first four parts…",
   "We have spent five parts in something close to admiration"). Required by the
   standalone rule; the *placement and cadence* is what repeats. Varying where it
   falls (P6 integrates it into the argument beautifully; P3 and P4 both park it
   in slot two) would hide the scaffolding.

3. **The "So what did I find" closer.** P1 ("So what did I find, going to look
   for the meaning under the fog?"), P2 ("So what is the birth of dharma, in the
   end?"), P3 ("So what did I find, in the end, in the teak country of Blora?"),
   P4 ("So what does the world of dharmas show us, taken whole?"). Four
   consecutive essays close their argument with the same rhetorical question and,
   in P1 and P3, the same anaphoric "I found… I found… I found" answer. P5–P8
   vary their endings and are the better for it. Recasting two of the four would
   keep the device special.
   _✓ Done 2026-07-06 (recommendation #6): P2 now opens its close with direct
   assertion ("The birth of dharma, in the end, is not a revelation. It is a
   response."), and P4 with "Taken whole, the world of dharmas shows human
   beings facing the one problem of how to live well together, and building
   answers of breathtaking variety." P1's and P3's closers — the two strongest —
   are untouched and now unique._

4. **The honesty ritual.** Every essay stages a formal self-caution: P1 (two
   questions left open), P2 (the Axial-Age caveat), P3 ("An Honest Pause"), P4
   (the shadow paragraph per dharma + Krech), P5 (the oversold-neuroscience
   discipline), P6 (the self-indictment on appropriation), P7 ("we should be
   suspicious of how readily the people running them… declare their own
   success"), P8 ("The Mirror"). **This is simultaneously the series' greatest
   distinction and a genuine tic.** It is the thing that earns the reader's
   trust, and it is also a beat the reader learns to anticipate — admire, then
   caveat, then resume — with vocabulary to match ("honest" and cognates ×32,
   "the honest answer/question/name" clustering in P7–P8). Do not cut the
   honesty; do vary its *staging* (P5's version, woven through the neuroscience
   rather than sectioned off, is the model).

5. **"The [discipline] [Name]" introduction formula.** Counts per essay: 4, 6,
   3, 6, 9, 10, 6, **20** (P1→P8). It is the correct sourcing idiom — the
   template mandates naming people in prose — but P8 introduces a new authority
   roughly every 280 words, and the formula's fixed syntax makes the density
   audible. In P8 especially, some names could be carried by their idea ("the
   orthogonality thesis holds that…", with the name in the sources list) without
   violating the sourcing rule. See §4 on P8 generally.

## 4. Does it read well?

**Sentence by sentence: yes, unusually well.** The prose is confident without
being ornamental, the concrete images (feet in cement, the deadpan on the forest
road, the funeral where the body has not received the memo, hearts synchronising
across a firewalk) are doing real argumentative work, and the sequencing of
evidence inside each essay is expert. P3 (the Samin) and P5 (installation) are
the two best essays: each has one subject, one schema, and room to breathe. P6
sustains a hard argument without hectoring. P7 is the tightest.

**Register: the year-12 claim is optimistic.** The frontmatter declares
`reading_level: year-12` on all nine. The vocabulary is plain, as promised, but
the sentence architecture — long paratactic chains with heavy apposition, often
three or four clauses deep — and the sheer density of named authorities put the
effective level nearer first-year university. The template licenses "a little
difficulty," so this is a labelling question more than a writing question: either
relax some of the longest sentences (P4's "What Rhymes" section and most of P8)
or adjust the declared level.

**Pacing across the series.** Parts 1–5 escalate well; P6 is the emotional peak
and knows it; P7 is short, urgent, and correctly so. **P8 is the one structural
concern.** It is the longest essay (5,590 words), the densest in citations (26
key works vs 6–13 elsewhere, 20 discipline-Name introductions vs 3–10), and it is
doing five jobs: planetary dharma, AI ethics + work/UBI, post-traditional
building, the Mirror (the project under its own microscope), and the
relativism resolution. The "flattening" schema does hold it together — the
template's named-schema rule is well used — but the middle of "The Mirror"
(Firth, Saussure, embedding-averages) rises to a level of abstraction the rest of
the series never asks of the reader, right before the finale needs them moved
rather than tired. Options, in ascending order of surgery: thin the authority
density; compress the Delphi/model-collapse/embedding triple to two mechanisms;
or accept the overshoot as the destination essay's licence (it is within the
±1,000 budget). The final two sections ("Plural, but Not Anything Goes" and
"That Which Holds") are excellent and should not be touched.

**The preface earns its keep.** P0 is a different register — warmer, more
exclamatory, personal — and that is right for a preface. The two disclosures that
matter (the AI-assisted method with human judgement; the corpus-lean confession)
are exactly what the series' credibility requires. Its one real cost is the
thesis pre-statement (§2 above). The two "(yes, the irony)" / "(yes, another
irony)" asides are a deliberate echo that works.

**The reader's trust.** The strongest overall quality of the series is that its
epistemics are part of its aesthetics: the source-caution *about* the Samin, the
misattributed-Aristotle catch, the failed-replication story told as a virtue of
science — the method keeps showing up inside the prose as narrative material.
That is rare, and it is the series' signature. Protect it in any tic-fixing pass:
several of the "honest" clusters are load-bearing.
