# 1.4 Cross-cultural Cognates

**Question:** How does dharma compare with cognate ordering concepts from other
traditions — Greek *nomos* and *logos*, Chinese *Tao*, Vedic *ṛta*, Egyptian *ma'at*,
and Western *natural law*? What is genuinely shared across them, and what is distinct
to each?

## Synthesis

Across the great civilisations, complex societies independently converged on a single
idea: that reality has an intrinsic **order** which fuses the cosmic, the social, and the
moral, and that human virtue consists in *aligning* with it while disorder (chaos, the lie,
anomie) consists in violating it [S1][S5][S6][S8]. Ṛta, Egyptian *ma'at*, Zoroastrian
*asha*, the Greek *logos*, the Chinese *Tao*, and the Western *natural law* are all variants
of this "cosmic-order" intuition — the same "that which holds" impulse traced etymologically
in [[1.1-etymology]] — and beneath their divergent cosmologies sits a convergent behavioural
kernel (the near-universal Golden Rule, reciprocity) [S4]. But they are **not all the same
kind of thing**: they separate along two axes — order *discovered* (ṛta, asha, ma'at, Tao,
logos, natural law) vs order *made* (Greek *nomos*, which is actually convention), and
order *personal/sacralised* (ma'at, asha, ṛta) vs *impersonal/rational* (Tao, logos, natural
law) [S9][S10][S11]. Dharma's distinctive contribution is a **two-level architecture** —
ṛta names the impersonal cosmic order, *dharma* names its application to human conduct and
role [S6][S7] — which is precisely why "dharma," unlike the others, ports so readily into
secular use: the tradition had already carved off the lived, this-worldly layer from the
cosmology.

## Findings

### A category correction first
The question (and the corpus, which lists "Ma'at, Kittu, Dao, Ṛta/Dharma" together as
"comprehensive orders... part cosmology, part law, part etiquette" [S1]) lumps two different
classes:
- **Cosmic-order cognates** — ṛta, ma'at, asha, Tao, logos, natural law: principles that an
  order *exists in reality* and conduct should conform to it.
- **Greek *nomos* is the outlier.** In the Greek *nomos / physis* debate, *physis* is nature
  (how things are independent of us) and *nomos* is **convention** — the human-made laws,
  customs, and moral codes that "vary between cultures and could have been otherwise"; the
  Sophists used exactly this to argue that justice is *nomos*, not *physis* [S9]. So *nomos*
  belongs with custom/law as dharma's **contrast** (see [[1.3-boundary-definition]]), not with
  the cosmic-order family. The true Greek cosmic-order cognate is **logos**.
- And social dharma-analogues like Ubuntu or Bushidō [S3] are lived *ethical systems*, not
  cosmic-order principles — kin to dharma-as-conduct, not to ṛta-as-cosmos.

### The cognates, compared

| Concept | Tradition | Core meaning | Distinctive accent |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ṛta → Dharma** | Vedic / Indic | ṛta = immutable cosmic order; dharma = its manifestation in living beings, duty, role [S6][S7] | **Two-level**: cosmic order *plus* a separate applied human-conduct layer keyed to role/stage of life |
| **Asha** (vs *druj*) | Zoroastrian | "How things have been fit together"; truth/righteousness/cosmic order [S8] | **Dualistic**: order as active combat between *asha* (truth) and *druj* (the Lie); direct etymological cognate of ṛta |
| **Ma'at** (vs *isfet*) | Ancient Egyptian | Truth, justice, balance, cosmic order, against chaos (*isfet*) [S5] | **Sacralised & political**: a goddess; pharaoh rules "in Ma'at"; post-mortem Weighing of the Heart |
| **Logos** | Greek (Heraclitus → Stoic) | The rational principle governing the cosmos; "all things happen according to logos" [S9] | **Rationalist/epistemic**: order = intelligibility; cosmos is logical and grasped by reason |
| **Tao / Dao** | Chinese (Daoist / Confucian) | The "Way": the flow that generates and sustains cosmic order [S10] | **Naturalistic/spontaneous**: alignment via *wu wei* (non-coercion); least personified, least legalistic |
| **Natural law** | Greco-Roman → Christian | Universal moral law knowable by reason; Cicero's *recta ratio*; Aquinas' participation in eternal law [S11] | **Propositional/universalist**: yields explicit, argued, universally-binding norms; basis for rights |
| *(Nomos)* | Greek | Human convention/law (the *physis* contrast) [S9] | *Not a cosmic-order cognate — the "made," relative pole* |

### What is genuinely shared
1. **Fusion of cosmic and moral order.** In each (bar nomos), the same principle governs the
   stars *and* conduct — there is no is/ought gap because the cosmos is already normative.
   Ma'at, ṛta, and asha each explicitly "function across physical, ethical, and ritual
   domains" [S6][S8]; logos is "both the immanent law of nature and the guiding principle of
   human conduct" [S9]; the Dao "governs cosmic processes and ethical conduct" [S10]; natural
   law makes morality a feature of the rational order of nature [S11].
2. **Virtue = alignment; vice = disorder.** Each pairs the order with its negation — *adharma*,
   *druj* (the Lie), *isfet* (chaos) — and casts the good life as conformity to the order and
   evil as its disruption [S5][S8].
3. **A discovered, not invented, standard.** All claim the order is *found* in reality, giving
   morality an authority beyond mere human stipulation — the very move *nomos* denies [S9][S11].
4. **A convergent behavioural kernel underneath.** Despite radically different cosmologies, the
   conduct they license rhymes: Sapolsky notes the **near-universal Golden Rule** (in positive
   "do unto others" and negative "do not" framings) and reciprocity as the common currency,
   plus Shweder's cross-cultural ethics of autonomy/community/divinity and Haidt's moral
   foundations [S4]. The cosmology varies; the cooperation it underwrites is broadly constant
   (cf. [[1.2-universal-patterns]]).

### What is distinct to each
- **Ṛta/dharma** alone builds an explicit *two-storey* system (impersonal cosmic ṛta + applied
  human dharma), and ties duty to social role and life-stage — the cosmic→social mapping is its
  signature [S6][S7].
- **Asha** frames order as *cosmic dualism* — a live war between truth and falsehood — more than
  any other; and is the closest *linguistic* relative of ṛta (both < PII *\*ṛtá-*) [S8].
- **Ma'at** is uniquely *personified and politically load-bearing*: cosmic order is a goddess,
  royal legitimacy depends on upholding her, and individual fate is judged against her feather
  [S5].
- **Logos** is the most *intellectualist*: order is reason/intelligibility, and the route to it
  is understanding rather than ritual or duty [S9].
- **Tao** is the most *naturalistic and non-coercive*: order is spontaneous flow, accessed by
  *not* forcing (*wu wei*); the Daoist accent on ultimate cosmic principle contrasts with the
  Confucian accent on deliberate ritual (*li*) and social hierarchy [S10].
- **Natural law** is the most *propositional and universalising*: it converts the cosmic order
  into argued, rationally-derivable rules binding on all rational agents — the lineage that
  feeds modern human-rights universalism [S11].

### Why this matters for the project
The recurrence of cosmic-order concepts across unconnected civilisations is the
**cosmological-scale evidence** for the universal pattern of [[1.2-universal-patterns]]: where
that note argued from the evolution of cooperation, this one shows the same impulse surfacing
as metaphysics. And dharma's two-level structure explains its special portability: a *secular*
dharma can keep the applied conduct-layer (compassion, situational ethics, cooperation) while
dropping or naturalising the cosmic-order metaphysics — a move far harder for ma'at (a goddess)
or natural law (participation in eternal law) than for the already-half-detached "dharma."

## Tensions, gaps & corpus bias

- **The predicted corpus gap was confirmed.** The shared corpus *named* the cognates and gave
  the ṛta link and the Golden-Rule kernel [S1][S2][S4], but supplied essentially no substantive
  content on nomos, logos, ma'at, Tao, or natural law. ~7 of this note's claims rest on external
  sources — the largest external-to-KB ratio of any Foundational note so far, exactly because
  the corpus is Indic- and secular-centric and weak on Egyptian, Chinese, Greek, and Christian
  primary material. [bias flag]
- **Equivalence is being asserted, partly by modern synthesisers.** Statements like "one can
  recognise Ma'at in all pagan societies; in India it is Rta and Dharma" [S6] risk a *perennialist*
  flattening — treating distinct concepts as one trans-cultural truth. The axes above are an
  attempt to resist that: real structural differences (personal vs impersonal; discovered vs
  made; one-level vs two-level) must not be dissolved into "they're all the same."
- **Translation distortion.** Each term is famously untranslatable; rendering ṛta, ma'at, Tao,
  and logos all as "cosmic order" imports a common frame that none of the traditions used of
  itself, and can over-state similarity (the same caution flagged for the dharman/themis parallel
  in [[1.1-etymology]]). The deeper reason is **Saussurean**: a term's meaning is its *value within
  its own system of contrasts* — "in the language there are only differences without positive
  terms" — so *ṛta* signifies by standing against *anṛta*, *ma'at* against *isfet*, and *Tao*
  against the forced and unnatural, in webs that do not line up; collapsing each into the shared
  English label "cosmic order" discards exactly those system-relative differences [S12].
- **Nomos's mis-listing is itself informative.** That the question grouped *nomos* (convention)
  with cosmic-order concepts shows how easily the "order" family absorbs its own opposite — a
  useful warning for [[3.4-relativism-universalism]], since the *nomos/physis* debate is the
  original relativism-vs-universalism dispute.
- **Cited via summaries.** Premnath's *Rta and Maat* comparison, the SEP entries (Daoism,
  Natural Law), Heraclitus' and the Stoics' *logos*, Cicero's *De Legibus*, and Aquinas are
  cited via encyclopedic/secondary sources, not the primary texts.
